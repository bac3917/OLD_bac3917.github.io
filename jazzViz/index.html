The jazzVIZ Project

Thousands of jazz albums produced in the United States included thousands of musicians. Who played with who? Who was the most central musician in 1964? Where did studios produce the most albums? The network maps and visualizations below help to illustrate an answer to these kinds of questions.

What you see here is the pilot phase of an ambitious project. While numerous websites store information about jazz albums and their artists who created them none permit for data analysis, manipulation, visualizations and/or extraction. When a full database of musicians, their albums, dates, studios and locations is developed, a great deal of questions can be thrown at it.
Some great jazz documentation has been done already. Projects like jazzdisco.org and discogs.org have organized a great deal of information. Ideally, after demonstrating some of the ideas introduced above, raw data from these and similar sources can be made available.
The purpose of the jazz database is to document American jazz through the 1970s. The goal is to create a set of "jazz data" in a universally available and systematic format that will ultimately be available as an education resource. When the database reaches a sufficient development level it will be released according to terms of a GNU Free Documentation License. There are several key steps along the way. First, a database design is needed, then the data must be collected, cleaned and organized. Concerning those data -- what will they look like? It might be best to think of them in terms of the "buckets" the database will use:
      • Artists: A list of American jazz musicians, with basic biographical data such as date of birth, date of death, birthplace.
      • Albums: A list of albums, from Kind of Blue to This is Duke Ellington. The album table will include a data for each artist who performed on it. Links to other tables table with Label Code (see below).
      • Network Connections: Simply a list of each artist and what other artists he or she worked with on a published album.
      • Labels: A list of record studios and the various labels each produced. This is a difficult set of data if not only because there was not any standardization in jazz label nomenclature until 1977 when the Label Code (LC) was introduced by the International Association of Sound and Audiovisual Archives (IASA), which was founded in 1969.

Verve Records

Verve Records was founded by Norman Granz in 1956 and includes recordings of legendary artists such as Ella Fitzgeralnd and Stan Getz.
