The jazzVIZ Project

Thousands of jazz albums produced in the United States included thousands of musicians. Who played with who? Who was the most central musician in 1964? Where did studios produce the most albums? The network maps and visualizations below help to illustrate an answer to these kinds of questions.

What you see here is the pilot phase of an ambitious project. While numerous websites store information about jazz albums and their artists who created them none permit for data analysis, manipulation, visualizations and/or extraction. When a full database of musicians, their albums, dates, studios and locations is developed, a great deal of questions can be thrown at it.

Some great jazz documentation has been done already. Projects like jazzdisco.org and discogs.org have organized a great deal of information. Ideally, after demonstrating some of the ideas introduced above, raw data from these and similar sources can be made available.

The purpose of the jazz database is to document American jazz through the 1970s. The goal is to create a set of "jazz data" in a universally available and systematic format that will ultimately be available as an education resource. When the database reaches a sufficient development level it will be released according to terms of a GNU Free Documentation License. There are several key steps along the way. First, a database design is needed, then the data must be collected, cleaned and organized.
