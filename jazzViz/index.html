The jazzVIZ Project

Thousands is an apt term for American jazz if you're thinking about representing it with data.

What musicians played with other musicians? Where did studios produce the most albums? When did bands overlap with studio recordings? Social network analysis, maps and timelines correspond to these kinds of questions.

What you see here is the pilot phase of an ambitious project. While numerous websites store information about jazz albums and their artists who created them (like jazzdisco.org and discogs.org) none permit for data analysis, manipulation, visualizations and/or extraction. When a full database of musicians, their albums, dates, studios and locations is developed, a great deal of questions can be thrown at it.

The purpose of the jazzViz project is to document American jazz from its inception through the 1960s. The goal is to create a set of "jazz data" in a universally available and systematic format that will ultimately be available as an education resource. When the database reaches a sufficient development level it will be released according to terms of a GNU Free Documentation License. There are several key steps along the way. First, a database design is needed, then the data must be collected, cleaned and organized. Lee Morgan's The Sidewinder

There is no argument that Lee Morgan's The Sidewinder is an iconic jazz album. The five musicians who made the album a reality were connected to many jazz greats. In the network graph below you can see their sphere of influence. Billy Higgins, Joe Henderson, Barry Harris and Morgan himself are among the most connected musicians who were personnel on this album.

Many JazzViz Possibilities

Within the social networks of jazz musicians are cliques: groupings of musicians who work frequently with one another and end up as sub-groups within a larger network. Some of these cliques overlap and others do not. In the context of jazz history, the cliques may represent opportunities for sharing new jazz styles or other cases insulating a genre of artists from another.
